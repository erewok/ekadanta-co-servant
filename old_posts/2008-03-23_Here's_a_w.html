So, Richard Whitlock wrote a thing called ", or observations on the present manners of the English" in year 1654, in which he wrote the following:

 Too severe Censurer (free of the Company of *Pish~mongers) that Pisheth at any thing not exact.

This may sound a little fluttery, but Whitlock was talking about "pish," the act of  "Expressing contempt, impatience, or disgust" or "treating with contempt and making light of" (according to the OED online). Someone who does such is (humorously, obsoletely, rarely) a pish-monger, or in other words, a hater, and don't forget to let that person know it.
